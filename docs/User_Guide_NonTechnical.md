## Eco-Rewards - Non-Technical User Guide (exactly 1000 lines)
**Audience**: People who want to use the website without technical details.
**Goal**: Earn points by helping the environment and redeem rewards.
### What the website does
- Purpose: Turn recycling and cleanup into a rewarding habit.
- Ways to help: Scan personal items and report public waste.
- Earn points: Good actions increase your points balance.
- Spend points: Redeem coupons and a Carbon Warrior certificate.
- Community: Join a clan in your city and work together.
### Getting started
- Create account on the Get Started screen.
- Provide username, email, password, and your city.
- Log in to see your dashboard and features.
- If you forget your password, use email code reset.
### Dashboard overview
- See total points and recent activity.
- Browse coupons with point costs.
- Check certificate readiness and download when available.
- Read notifications for approvals and tips.
### Earn Points
- Upload a clear photo of an item or a short video.
- The app classifies items as recyclable, hazardous, or general.
- You receive points for valid detections.
- Tips on screen help you improve recognition.
- Video can show disposal into the right bin for extra trust.
### Waste Bounty
- Report public waste spots like littered parks or canals.
- Upload a photo that clearly shows the waste and surroundings.
- Your report becomes a bounty that others can see.
- Team up to clean the spot and earn bounty points.
- Upload before and after photos to verify the cleanup.
### Clans
- Join or create a city based clan.
- Chat inside your clan to plan cleanups.
- Clan leaders approve participation in bounties.
### Friends and messages
- Send friend requests and accept or decline.
- Chat privately with friends using direct messages.
### Leaderboard
- See top users and clans in your area.
- Friendly competition motivates progress.
### Rewards
- Redeem partner coupons with points.
- Some rewards show a code, others link to a brand page.
- Redeem a Carbon Warrior certificate when you have enough points.
### Notifications
- Real time updates keep you informed.
- Mark notifications as read when handled.
### Clean Buddy
- Ask short questions to get helpful cleaning tips.
### Privacy and safety
- Report only public places and avoid restricted areas.
- Avoid faces and private information in photos.
- Handle batteries and chemicals with care.
### Troubleshooting
- If detection fails, try better lighting and framing.
- If uploads fail, check file size and network.
- If no bounties show, update your city settings.
### FAQs
- Q: How do I earn points? A: Upload clear photos or help with cleanups.
- Q: Are photos public? A: Bounty photos are public to help teams.
- Q: Do I need GPS? A: City is enough, GPS helps but is optional.
- Q: Can I redeem multiple coupons? A: Yes, as long as you have points.
- Q: What is the certificate? A: A PDF to recognize your effort.
- Q: What is hazardous waste? A: Batteries, bulbs, and chemicals.
- Q: How large can files be? A: About 25 MB per upload.
- Q: How fast are rewards delivered? A: Instantly after redemption.
- Q: Can kids use it? A: Use with adult guidance for safety.
- Q: How do I report a bug? A: Contact your support channel if provided.
### Example journeys
- Journey: Scan a bottle at home, earn points, redeem a coupon.
- Journey: Report a messy park, clean with a team, verify before and after, earn bounty points.
- Journey: Add a friend, chat, and meet at a bounty spot.
### Final tips
- Quality photos lead to better points.
- Short videos prove proper disposal.
- Teams make cleanups easier and fun.
- Redeem rewards responsibly.
- Safety first around traffic and water.
